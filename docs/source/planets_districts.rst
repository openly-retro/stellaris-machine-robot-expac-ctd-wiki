Planets and Districts
#####################

World-Machines
**************

World-Machines are fully automated Machine planets. Districts and buildings do not require any pops for production of resources. The planets themselves can be considered themselves as Machine units.

The standard World Machine is the **Auto-Machine World**, which produces all raw resources for the empire.


Ascension perk vs Origin
------------------------

**AP:**

Machine empire players that choose the :ref:`ap_world_machines` ascension perk  will be able to upgrade their Auto-Machine Worlds to advanced planet classes without any extra steps. The primary district will provide housing for pops.

New World Machines must be started after a colony has been founded.

Colonizable planets can be transformed into the four types of World-Machine, using a planetary decision costing 20,000 minerals, 200 influence. It takes 3600 days to complete.

**Origin:**

Players who start their Machine empire with the :ref:`origin_world_machines` origin will go through special projects and technology advancements in order to unlock advanced world-Machine planet classes. The primary district will _not_ provide housing for pops.

New World Machines must be started by planetary decision on an uncolonized habitable world (or barren world).

Colonizable planets can be transformed into the four types of World-Machine, using a planetary decision costing 20,000 minerals, 200 influence. It takes 3600 days to complete.



Auto-Machine World Project
--------------------------
An automated planet useful for producing raw resources.

**Districts (Stellaris 4.x+):**

- Primary district starts with an Alloys zone
- Auto-Mining Machine, Auto-Farm, Auto-Generator: Each have specializations to add a bit of research, or further produce more of a given resource

**Districts (Stellaris 3.14):**

- City/Nexus: Houses pops
- Auto-Mining Machine, Auto-Farm, Auto-Generator: Produces Minerals, Food, and Energy
- Auto-Industrial Machine: Produces alloys/CGs


Auto-Factory World Project
--------------------------

An automated planet useful for producing secondary resources, like alloys, consumer goods, and rare resources.

**Districts (Stellaris 4.x+):**

- Primary District (Cooling): Produces alloys
- Production district: Specializes to produce Alloys or Consumer Goods
- Rare Resources district: Specializes to produce any or all of the three main strategics
- Military district: Specializes to 1) add naval cap, 2) ground defenses to the planet the district is built on, or 3) improve empire ship speed

**Districts (Stellaris 3.14):**

- Cooling District
- Alloys
- Goods
- Rare Resources
- Naval Cap

Auto-Processor World Project
----------------------------
For your empire's research needs: a specialized computing planet.

**Districts (Stellaris 4.x+):**

- Primary District (Cooling)
- Circuit Unit Pi: Produces physics research
- Circuit Unit Sigma: Produces society research
- Circuit Unit Epsilon: Produces engineering research

**Districts (Stellaris 3.14):**

- Cooling district
- Circuit Unit Pi: Produces physics research
- Circuit Unit Sigma: Produces society research
- Circuit Unit Epsilon: Produces engineering research
- Decryption district: Improves empire decryption strength


Auto-Server World Project
-------------------------
A world specialized for handling administrative overhead

**Districts (Stellaris 4.x+):**

- Primary district: Cooling
- Unity district
- Logistics Districg
- Empire-wide anti-crime district

**Districts (Stellaris 3.14):**

- Cooling district
- Drive: Produces unity.
- Router: Produces Logistics
- Firewall: Empire-wide deviancy -5 per district

Auto-Ringworld
--------------

A more powerful version of a Ringworld, and can produce significant research, alloys, and resources.

**Districts (Stellaris 4.x+):**

- Primary District, can be specialized
- R&D District: Support producing either Unity, or Research
- Industrial District: Produce Alloys or Consumer Goods
- Raw Resource district: Produce Food, or Energy

**Districts (Stellaris 3.14):**

(Does not have a cooling district because Ringworld segments are limited in size)

- Auto-Generator
- Auto-Farm
- Auto-Commercial: Produces trade (non-gestalt only)
- Auto-Science: Produces research
- Auto-Industrial: Produces alloys and/or consumer goods depending on colony designation


Colony designations
-------------------

Each world-machine type has several colony designations available, set up generally to help players focus on stacking a particular resource. There are also "standard" automation settings for each world-machine, which will build districts fairly evenly, including cooling districts.

Using the colony automations with auto-machine worlds is a great quality-of-life improvement for players who don't want much planetary micromanagement.

*As of Phoenix 4.0 the colony automations don't work anymore sadly, we have to wait for Paradox to fix this in the base game*

Troubleshooting planetary automation in 3.14: You cannot have open jobs on a planet for the automation to start building districts.


Advanced Planetary Mechanics
----------------------------

All World-Machines, except the non-specialized **Auto-Machine World** will overheat, reducing resource output on the planet, unless a **Cooling System** district is built to counteract the **Thermal Throttling** effect. The specialized districts of each type **do not require pops** to provide their bonuses.


Mechanical Worlds
*****************

**Robotic species or Synthetic Ascended origin only**

The Mechanical world is meant to be a parallel to Machine Worlds, for Synthetic empires.

The Mechanical World is a terraforming option, not a decision like the auto-machine world project. Once terraformed, the Mechanical world provides new districts, and job slots.

**Districts (Stellaris 4.x+):**

- City District: Provides housing, clerk jobs, improves ruler pop resource output by 1%, improves specialist pop resource output by 1%
- Generator District: provides housing, technician jobs. Improves energy credits from technicians by 1%
- Mining District: Provides housing, mining jobs. Improves minerals from miners by 1%
- Agriculture district: Provides housing, farming jobs. Improves food from farmers by 1%

**Districts (Stellaris 3.14+):**

- City District (or Nexus), providing housing. improves ruler pop resource output by 1%, improves specialist pop resource output by 1%
- Industrial district: Consumer goods, or alloys, or both
- Generator: Improves energy credits from technicians by 1%
- Mining: Improves minerals from miners by 1%
- Farming: Improves food from farmers by 1%

Virtual Reality Server
**********************

**Available for the Mechanical origin (Utopia)**

The Virtual Reality server converts pops into planetary trade value by way of virtualisation.
Additionally, the planet has districts for boosting empire-wide amenities, entertainment, and other bonuses.

**Districts (Stellaris 4.x+):**

- Primary District: Produces Trade
- Secondary districts: Research, Unity, Amenities

**Districts (Stellaris 3.14+):**

- Essential: This district is tied to the matter-converter, which converts pops to trade value.
- Research
- Trade
- Admin (Unity)
- Amenities (empire-wide)
